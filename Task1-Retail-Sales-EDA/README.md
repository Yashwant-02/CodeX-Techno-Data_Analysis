# 🛒 Exploratory Data Analysis on Retail Sales Data

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a retail sales dataset to uncover trends, patterns, and business insights that support data-driven decision-making. The dataset includes sales transactions, customer demographics, product categories, and regional data.

---

## 📁 Dataset Information

- **Rows**: 7,394  
- **Columns**: 15  
- **Format**: CSV  
- **Source**: Hypothetical retail store transactions

### Key Columns:
- `Order Date`
- `Product ID`
- `Product Category`
- `Quantity`
- `Total Sales`
- `Buyer Gender`
- `Buyer Age`
- `Order Location`
- `Rating`
- `Review`

---

## 🧰 Tools & Libraries Used

- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

---

## 🔍 Key Analysis Performed

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Converted `Order Date` to datetime
- Removed duplicates
- Checked and handled missing values

### 2. Descriptive Statistics
- Summary stats for numerical & categorical data
- Product category & gender distribution

### 3. Time Series Analysis
- Monthly total sales trends plotted using line charts

### 4. Customer and Product Analysis
- Top 10 best-selling products by quantity
- Top 10 highest sales by order
- Age distribution of buyers
- City-wise revenue performance

### 5. Visualizations
- Bar plots, histograms, line graphs using Matplotlib & Seaborn

### 6. Correlation Analysis
- Quantity vs Total Sales correlation calculation

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- 📅 **January** had the highest number of orders, indicating a strong start-of-year buying trend.
- 👕 **Clothing** was the top-selling product category by revenue.
- 📍 **Sydney** generated the highest sales among all cities.
- 👤 The most common buyer age was **34**, showing a dominant middle-aged customer base.
- 📊 The correlation between `Quantity` and `Total Sales` was **0.63**, suggesting a strong positive relationship.
- 💰 The total revenue from all orders was **₹856,462**.
